The Oakland Public Education Fund

A trusted institutional home for educational initiatives in Oakland.

The Oakland Public Education Fund is a nonprofit organization whose mission is to lead the development and investment of community resources in Oakland public schools so that all students can learn, grow, and thrive. Founded in 2003, the Ed Fund has managed over $100 million in grants and donations in support of Oakland public schools, and serves as a trusted fiscal and administrative partner to educational initiatives across the Bay Area.

The Ed Fund is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization, providing the institutional infrastructure—financial management, grant compliance, reporting, and administrative oversight—that allows innovative initiatives like Futureschool Collaborative to operate with full legal and financial legitimacy from day one.

More information about the Oakland Public Education Fund is available at oaklandedfund.org.

Institutional structure

How Futureschool Collaborative is structured within the Ed Fund.

Futureschool Collaborative operates as a fiscally sponsored project of the Ed Fund, meaning that all grants, donations, and financial activity flow through the Ed Fund’s legal entity and are subject to its financial controls and reporting standards.

This structure is common in the education and philanthropic sectors, particularly for initiatives in earlier stages of development. It allows Futureschool Collaborative to receive tax-deductible contributions, apply for foundation grants that require 501(c)(3) sponsorship, and operate with full financial accountability—without the administrative overhead or burden.

For funders, this means all contributions to Futureschool Collaborative are made to the Oakland Public Education Fund, are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law, and are subject to the Ed Fund’s established financial management and compliance infrastructure.
